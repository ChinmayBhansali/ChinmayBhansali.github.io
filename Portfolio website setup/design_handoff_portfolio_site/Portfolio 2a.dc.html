Reducing household food waste

A mobile app that combines a grocery list, an inventory tracker, and a recipe finder — designed from field work, two conceptual models, and in-person usability testing.

hero: final app screens

The problem

Field work surfaced two reasons households waste food: forgetting an item exists in the pantry, and not knowing how to use it before it expires. We set three design goals — visibility, efficiency, and integration into daily routine — and chose mobile because it's the closest device to a daily habit.

Conveyor → Factory

Our first conceptual model was a conveyor belt: items ride toward their expiry date. Cognitive walkthroughs showed it explained time well but couldn't connect to recipe tasks — so it grew into a factory: the grocery list as bulletin board, the pantry as loading zone, the recipe finder as factory floor.

fig 1: conveyor model
fig 2: factory model

Testing with 11 people

In-person think-aloud sessions followed by a questionnaire (Likert + open-ended), against three research questions on efficiency, integration, and visibility. Piloting reshaped the study itself: we split one research question into two, merged two surveys into one, and swapped interviews for a questionnaire.
