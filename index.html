About Me

Hello! I'm Chinmay

I’m an Integrated Sciences student at the University of British Columbia, where I blend Computer Science, Psychology, and Mathematics to research the conversation between system capabilities and human needs. My vision is to democratize the psychological benefits of competitive sports by creating physically and socially grounded experiences within the Metaverse. By leveraging Self-Determination Theory, I aim to design virtual environments that satisfy core human needs for autonomy, competence, and relatedness.

I specialize in building intuitive systems that bridge the gap between digital and physical worlds. My current research at the UBC SPIN Lab involves developing Unity-based multiplayer prototypes that use robot swarms to facilitate remote social touch. My technical experience ranges from engineering ECS-based game engines in C++ and OpenGL to implementing real-time gesture recognition with TensorFlow and building multi-modal AI models for medical treatment prediction.

Outside of coding, I’m deeply involved in music and leadership roles at UBC. As a pianist and vocalist with Sargam-UBC, I've helped organize successful cultural events and performances. I also co-founded VHMUN, an inclusive Model United Nations conference aimed at empowering students through diplomacy and public speaking.

I am driven by a user-centered design process, iterating from formative fieldwork and affinity diagramming to high-fidelity prototyping and data-driven evaluation. I’m always eager to collaborate on technology that enhances human connection and solves meaningful challenges. Feel free to connect! I’m always open to new ideas and opportunities!
