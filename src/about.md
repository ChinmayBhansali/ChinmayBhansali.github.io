Alright, let's do this one last time. My name is Chinmay Bhansali. I grew up in Rajasthan, India, in schools where electronics were banned, and when my friends wanted a game we had nothing to play it on, so I drew one: a Pokémon RPG on notebook paper, with hand-drawn maps and battles tracked in pencil. I spent more time watching my friends play it than playing it myself, wondering what had passed between my mind and theirs through nothing but paper. Everything I do now is that question with better instruments.

At UBC I designed my own degree under Integrated Sciences, combining computer science, psychology, and mathematics: one to build systems, one to understand the people using them, one to measure what would otherwise stay invisible. Together they gave my curiosity its proper name, human-computer interaction. Along the way I studied [technology-mediated social touch](https://www.cs.ubc.ca/labs/spin/) at the SPIN Lab, and I'm now researching [AI use policy for classrooms](https://www.cs.ubc.ca/labs/socius/) at the SOCIUS Lab while TAing the HCI course where I ran my first study.

I graduated this spring, with Distinction, and next comes graduate research. The question I keep returning to: as AI makes more of our interface decisions, who notices when those decisions quietly work against the people using them? I'm choosing where to pursue that now, and the whole world is open.

Away from research I'm the pianist and a vocalist for [Sargam UBC](https://www.instagram.com/sargamubc/)'s annual Raagini concert, a sunset chaser with a phone camera, and the keeper of a [garden](/garden/) of unfinished thoughts. If one of them catches you, [tell me](mailto:chinmaybhansali29@gmail.com).
