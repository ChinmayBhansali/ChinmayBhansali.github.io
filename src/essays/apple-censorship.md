> **Prompt:** Many of the most popular apps in the world have been removed from the App Store in China to comply with government regulations. Is it ethical for Apple to remove apps from its App Store at the request of foreign governments in order to continue doing business there?

It is ethically problematic for Apple to remove apps from its App Store at the request of foreign governments, such as China, when doing so compromises fundamental rights like freedom of expression and access to information. Using Rule Utilitarianism, we can evaluate whether the universal adoption of Apple's policy would lead to greater overall happiness or harm.

Apple has removed VPNs, social media platforms, and news apps from its Chinese App Store to comply with government regulations. These apps are essential tools for accessing uncensored information and communicating freely. Their removal directly affects millions of users' ability to bypass censorship and engage with global content.

Rule Utilitarianism holds that we should adopt moral rules that, if universally followed, would maximize happiness for all affected parties. A rule such as "A company should comply with government requests to remove apps if it ensures continued business operations" may benefit the company and the host government. However, if universally adopted, this rule would empower authoritarian regimes to suppress dissent and restrict access to information globally, leading to widespread harm.

One might argue that Apple's compliance is necessary to maintain its presence in China, which allows it to provide other services and products that benefit Chinese consumers and sustain its global business.

While continued business operations may offer short-term benefits, the long-term consequences of legitimizing censorship are more severe. If all companies adopted this rule, it would normalize the suppression of digital rights, reduce global access to information, and erode trust in technology providers. The harms (loss of freedom, increased surveillance, and diminished innovation) outweigh the benefits.

Under Rule Utilitarianism, Apple's decision to remove apps at the request of foreign governments is unethical. The universal adoption of such a policy would lead to greater harm than good, undermining digital freedom and human rights. Ethical corporate behavior should prioritize long-term societal well-being over short-term compliance and profit.
